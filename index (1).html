About

This site documents a philosophical project in progress — a trilogy, a series of papers, and these demonstrations — all developing a single argument from different angles.

The central question: what is the relationship between meaning and reality, and what does the answer imply for artificial intelligence? The working answer runs through everything here. Meaning is more primordial than information. Before a system can process, optimize, or align — there must be a field in which distinctions are possible at all. That field is meaning. Artificial systems operate within it. They do not constitute it.

The practical implication is direct. Control over autonomous systems cannot be achieved through alignment alone. It requires interruptive authority — the retained standing to suspend continuation from outside the system's own evaluative framework. This is not an engineering problem. It is a structural one. And it has a structural solution.
